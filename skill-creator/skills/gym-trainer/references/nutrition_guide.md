# Nutrition Guide for Fitness Goals

## Macronutrient Fundamentals

### Protein
**Importance:** Muscle repair, growth, immune function
**Daily Requirements:**
- General health: 0.8g per kg bodyweight
- Active individuals: 1.2-1.6g per kg bodyweight
- Muscle building: 1.6-2.2g per kg bodyweight
- Fat loss: 2.2-2.6g per kg bodyweight

**Quality Sources:**
- Lean meats (chicken, turkey, lean beef)
- Fish and seafood
- Eggs and egg whites
- Dairy (Greek yogurt, cottage cheese)
- Plant-based (tofu, tempeh, legumes)

### Carbohydrates
**Importance:** Primary energy source, brain function, glycogen storage
**Daily Requirements:**
- General health: 3-5g per kg bodyweight
- Active training: 5-7g per kg bodyweight
- Endurance training: 7-10g per kg bodyweight

**Quality Sources:**
- Whole grains (oats, brown rice, quinoa)
- Fruits and vegetables
- Legumes and beans
- Starchy vegetables (sweet potatoes, regular potatoes)

### Fats
**Importance:** Hormone production, vitamin absorption, cell function
**Daily Requirements:** 0.8-1.2g per kg bodyweight (20-35% of total calories)

**Quality Sources:**
- Avocados and nuts
- Olive oil and coconut oil
- Fatty fish (salmon, mackerel, sardines)
- Seeds (chia, flax, pumpkin)

## Meal Timing Strategies

### Pre-Workout Nutrition (1-3 hours before)
**Goals:** Provide energy, prevent hunger, support performance
**Composition:** Carbs + Protein + Small amount of fat
**Examples:**
- Oatmeal with protein powder and fruit
- Banana with peanut butter and whey protein
- Greek yogurt with granola and berries

### Post-Workout Nutrition (within 2 hours)
**Goals:** Replenish glycogen, repair muscle tissue, rehydrate
**Composition:** Protein + Carbs + Fluids
**Examples:**
- Protein shake with banana
- Grilled chicken with rice and vegetables
- Greek yogurt with honey and nuts

### Daily Meal Distribution
**Option 1: 3 Main Meals + 2-3 Snacks**
- Breakfast, Lunch, Dinner
- Mid-morning and afternoon snacks
- Pre/post-workout nutrition as needed

**Option 2: Intermittent Fasting**
- 8-hour eating window, 16-hour fast
- Adjust meal timing around training

## Hydration Guidelines

### Daily Water Needs
- Minimum: 2-3 liters per day
- Active individuals: 3-4 liters per day
- Hot climates/high sweat: 4-6 liters per day

### Signs of Dehydration
- Dark yellow urine
- Fatigue or dizziness
- Headache
- Dry mouth

### Electrolyte Replacement
Important during intense/long workouts:
- Sodium: Sports drinks, salty foods
- Potassium: Bananas, oranges, potatoes
- Magnesium: Nuts, seeds, leafy greens

## Healthy Recipe Ideas

### High-Protein Breakfast
**Protein Oatmeal**
- 1/2 cup oats cooked in water
- 1 scoop whey protein mixed in
- 1 tablespoon almond butter
- Berries and cinnamon

### Muscle-Building Lunch
**Chicken Quinoa Bowl**
- 150g grilled chicken breast
- 1 cup cooked quinoa
- Mixed vegetables (broccoli, peppers, onions)
- Olive oil and lemon dressing

### Recovery Dinner
**Salmon & Sweet Potato**
- 200g baked salmon
- 200g roasted sweet potato
- Steamed broccoli
- Side salad with olive oil dressing

### Post-Workout Snack
**Greek Yogurt Parfait**
- 200g Greek yogurt
- 1/2 cup mixed berries
- 1 tablespoon chia seeds
- 1 tablespoon honey

## Supplements (When Needed)

### Evidence-Based Supplements
- **Whey Protein:** Convenient protein source
- **Creatine Monohydrate:** Strength and muscle gains
- **Vitamin D:** Immune and bone health
- **Omega-3 Fish Oil:** Heart and joint health
- **Multivitamin:** Insurance against deficiencies

### Timing Considerations
- **Protein:** Anytime, especially post-workout
- **Creatine:** 5g daily, with food
- **Vitamin D:** With largest meal of the day
- **Omega-3:** With meals to improve absorption

## Special Considerations

### Vegetarian/Vegan Diets
- **Protein Combining:** Legumes + grains for complete proteins
- **Vitamin B12:** Supplement required
- **Iron:** Plant sources + vitamin C for absorption
- **Omega-3:** Flaxseeds, chia seeds, algae supplements

### Food Allergies/Intolerances
- **Dairy:** Use lactose-free or plant-based alternatives
- **Gluten:** Choose gluten-free grains (rice, quinoa, oats)
- **Nuts:** Substitute seeds for healthy fats

### Budget-Friendly Options
- **Batch Cooking:** Prepare meals in advance
- **Seasonal Produce:** Buy fruits/vegetables in season
- **Bulk Purchases:** Buy staples in larger quantities
- **Home Cooking:** Prepare meals at home more often
